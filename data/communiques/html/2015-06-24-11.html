      The European Council

      \n

      The European Council is the EU institution that defines the general political direction and priorities of the European Union. It consists of the heads of state or government of the member states, together with its President and the President of the Commission.\xc2\xa0

      The Council of the European Union

      \n

      The Council of the EU is the institution representing the member states\' governments. Also known informally\xc2\xa0as the EU Council, it is where\xc2\xa0national ministers from each EU country\xc2\xa0meet to adopt laws and coordinate policies.

      Meetings

      \n

      The European Council brings together EU leaders at least four times a year.

      \n

      The Council of the EU meets\xc2\xa0in different configurations depending on the topic discussed.

      \n

      Committees and\xc2\xa0working parties handle the preparatory work on files before they are discussed at Council meetings.

\r\n Exit doorstep remarks by Jeroen Dijsselbloem following the Eurogroup meeting of 24 June 2015\r\n

\r\n\r\n \r\n\r\n \r\n
\r\n
\r\n
\r\n \r\n \r\n\r\n\r\n \r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n\r\n

The Eurogroup was informed about the state of play in the talks between the institutions and the Greek authorities.

There is no agreement yet.

Work will go on and the Eurogroup will reconvene Thursday, 25 June at 13:00 pm in order to prepare for the European Council.
